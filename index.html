Safety Zone: Safe (8/32")

Excellent wet grip, minimal braking distance. Perfect for highway driving.

6/32" to 10/32" — Optimal Safety

Excellent traction, low risk of hydroplaning, maximum braking efficiency.

3/32" to 5/32" — Warning Zone

Reduced traction in heavy rain. Schedule rotation immediately to ensure even wear patterns.

2/32" or Less — Critical / Illegal

Legally bald. Wet stopping distance increased by over 120ft. Tires must be replaced immediately.

Our Story & Safety Commitment

Taking high-end tire care out of the brick-and-mortar garage and putting it on wheels.

Safety In Every Thread, Precision In Every Turn

TireGlide was founded in 2024 by a group of automotive engineers and software developers who realized that standard tire maintenance took too much customer time and was prone to mechanic error. By designing custom service vans equipped with digital diagnostic tread depths scanners, certified dynamic wheel balancing drums, and automated torque controls, we set a new benchmark for tire services.

We operate with a single core principle: safety. Tires are the only contact point between your vehicle and the road. Proper rotation and balancing prevent uneven tread wear, increase stopping distances in wet conditions, and prevent high-speed blowouts.

Inspection Checkpoints

Our 10-Point Safety Protocol

Safety is not just a marketing word at TireGlide. Every tire service requires the technician to complete our mandatory 10-point checklist before final handoff. This includes digital hub rust removal, TPMS sensor verification, bead lubricity audits, dual-stage torque wrench validation to exact factory specs, and dynamic road force simulation tests. You receive the completed report in your customer portal.

Environmental Responsibility

Eco-Friendly Disposal & Recycling

We are dedicated to sustainable environmental practices. When we perform tire swaps or replace worn casings, we ensure the old tires are managed responsibly. Through partnerships with regional recycling centers, 100% of collected tire casings are processed into eco-friendly raw materials—such as safety crumb rubber for school playgrounds, asphalt rubber for green highways, and structural civil barriers.

Commercial Operations

Road Hazard Fleet Accounts

Minimize downtime for your delivery vehicles, sales team, and cargo trucks. TireGlide provides custom corporate fleet pricing and priority on-site routing scheduling. We service all light-commercial delivery vans, utility trucks, and passenger vehicles directly in your corporate lot or transit yard between shifts, complete with automated safety log compliance reports.

Sensor Calibration

TPMS Diagnostic Calibration

Is your dashboard warning light blinking? A malfunctioning Tire Pressure Monitoring System (TPMS) poses a safety risk. Our technicians carry specialized wireless diagnostic programming tools. We check sensor battery states, reset receiver chips, and replace worn TPMS valves or program brand-new sensors on-site for both domestic and import vehicles.

High Speed Calibration

Performance Wheel Balancing

For sports cars, high-end electric vehicles, and racing enthusiasts, standard balancing is not enough. Our mobile vans utilize road force dynamic balancing systems. By simulating high-speed road conditions inside our custom workshop unit, we find microscopic structural weight deviations, ensuring a completely smooth vibration-free steering response up to track speeds.

Locker Storage

Seasonal Tire Storage Solutions

Don't crowd your garage with offseason tire sets. When we perform your seasonal tire swap (winter to summer or vice versa), we can tag, clean, and transport your secondary tire set to our off-site climate-controlled warehouse. Your tires are stored away from ozone and moisture, and logged in your online profile for easy dispatch when seasonal swaps are needed.

Roadside Safety

Suburban Emergency Flat Repair

If you pick up a nail, screw, or road debris in your tire, don't attempt to drive on it. Our dispatch center can route a mobile unit to your location. We dismount the tire, perform a full safety inspection, clean the puncture channel, and apply a high-quality patch-plug combination from the inside—the only repair method approved by the Tire Industry Association.
